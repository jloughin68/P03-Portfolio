The Concept

Since this was our first project in 3D, we decided to make life easier for ourselves by sticking to non-organic shapes. In short: robots. Inspired by Pixar’s Wall-e and Overwatch: The Last Bastion, we decided upon a story featuring a small lovable robot who goes on a journey and makes a friend.

To consolidate our ideas into a feasible story, I led the team in creating a shot list. Quinn proceeded to illustrate it using our decided-upon robot designs, and Rajvir and Eleanor created a video with a voiceover in order to get us started thinking about timing.

Animatic

Using Quinn’s concept art as a guide, Rajvir and I modelled, textured, and lit our environments while Quinn modelled and textured our characters. The three of us then blocked out basic character movement, took still frames, and assembled the animatic video in order establish roughly what shots would be required for the final.

production

Animation & Communication

As Quinn, Rajvir, and I animated the film, we fired our animations back and forth over a group chat in screen-capture videos for critique. I discovered through this process that I enjoy animation greatly, as it reminds me of musical composition.

Rendering & Roadblocks

As we rendered, we came across some two issues: first, the reflectivity of the materials and the soft shadows meant that rendering took over 24 hours across many computers. And second, the small robot’s eye stopped glowing in some shots of the last scene. We made this discovery after it was too late to re-render—this was a learning experience for us, and a mistake that we will not repeat.

post-production

Sound

I led the sound design process; I discovered throughout this aspect of the project that sound design is far more involved for an animated short than for live-action, since there is absolutely no native sound to manipulate.

takeaways

One vital thing that I took away from this project was that I do not always need to be in charge of creative direction in order to be happy with a final product; until I worked on Crushed with Quinn, I hadn’t been part of a team dynamic in which I did take charge. I found the experience rather freeing, and it has allowed me to let go of some of my need for control in group projects.
