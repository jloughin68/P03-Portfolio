Crushed

A short film about a little robot's adventure, created by a team of five with Autodesk Maya over two months.

pre-production

Script, Concept Art, & Precedents

I wrote a script based on Quinn’s concept of a little robot’s journey; meanwhile, Quinn created the concept art, and the other members found precedents for style and story in Wall-e and The Last Bastion.

Shot-List & Storyboard

Next, I led the team in creating a shot-list, and Quinn painted our storyboard based upon it; I wrote the voice-over, and the others assembled our storyboard video.

Animatic

Quinn modelled and textured our characters while Rajvir and I modelled, textured, and lit our environment. The three of us then blocked out basic character movement, took still frames, and assembled the video.

production

Animation & Communication

As Quinn, Rajvir, and I animated the film, sending our animations back and forth over a group chat in screen-capture videos for critique. I discovered through this process that I enjoy animation greatly, as it reminds me of musical composition.

Rendering & Roadblocks

In this stage, we came across some two issues: first, the reflectivity of the materials and the soft shadows meant that rendering took over 24 hours across many computers. And second the small robot’s eye stopped glowing in some shots of the last scene. We made this discovery after it was too late to re-render the scene—this was a learning experience for us, and a mistake that we will not repeat.

Post-Production

Effects

With one day left to finish our film, Quinn worked on the colour correcting and spark effects, using me as a second pair of eyes.

Sound

I led the sound design process; I discovered throughout this aspect of the project that sound design is far more involved for an animated short than for live-action, since there is absolutely no native sound to manipulate.

takeaways

One vital thing that I took away from this project was that I do not always need to be in control in order to be happy with a final product; until I worked on Crushed with Quinn, I hadn’t been part of a team dynamic in which I was not the leader. I found the experience rather freeing, and it has allowed me to let go of some of my need for control in group projects.
