Ribbonbound

A multi-linear point-and-click experience with a painterly style and a whimsical urban fantasy theme, created by a team of two over three months. Made for a class called IAT 313-- Narrative and New Media.

pre-production

The Concept

We wanted to create as immersive a story as possible, and it seemed that the best way to do so would be interactive environmental storytelling—in short, an investigation-based narrative. In an attempt to create an entirely unique storyworld, we decided upon a post-apocalyptic, urban version of Hans Christian Anderson’s The Red Shoes, replete with hints of Irish folklore.

ORGANIZATION & COMMUNICATION

One organizational tool which proved extremely useful throughout the project was the Design Document, which acted as a masterlist for each object, environment, and character, as well as its purpose within the story.

Communication was also vital for such an intricate project. One specific instance of this need for communication occurred when Dom had a vision for Redhill street which did not quite match my own. Through discussion and some quick sketches, we were able to hash out exactly what we wanted and move forward with production.

Story

As the writer in the group, I decided to give the story a sense of dark whimsy, punctuated with humour and delivered to users through pop-up blurbs and multi-linear dialogue. One thing that helped to keep the intricate fold-back storyline with its many dependencies straight in my head was the use a table structure for the script, as well as flow-charts and storyboards.

Painting

Since I was painting characters and objects which had to fit into Dom’s art style, it was difficult to achieve a unified looking narrative. This required a great deal of trial and error with object/character placement, and a lot of editing each object/character once it was placed within the environment.
