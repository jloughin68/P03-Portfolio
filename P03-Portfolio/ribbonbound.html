Ribbonbound

A multi-linear point-and-click experience with a painterly style and a whimsical urban fantasy theme, created by a team of two over three months.

the team

Dominic (Dom) Quon

  • Worldbuilding, organization
  • Environment painting, animation
  • HTML & JavaScript

Juliana Loughin

  • Worldbuilding, story mapping, dialogue writing, character design
  • Character & interactable object painting, animation
  • Art direction, music & sound design

pre-production

Concept

We adapted a story I had already written for film into an interactive medium: a post-apocalyptic, urban version of Hans Christian Anderson’s The Red Shoes, replete with hints of Irish folklore.

Bible

We created a detailed write-up specifying story world and characters; I led this process, as I was the practiced storyteller in the group, and love world building and character design.

Design Document

We compiled a detailed list of every environment, character, and object, their functionalities within the story, and the assets we would need to represent them.

Painting

I created the objects and people while Dom worked with my feedback to refine the backgrounds. This was the most difficult portion of the project, as we had to meld together our two painting styles and match our lighting.

I arranged a medley of fiddle tunes, played it, recorded it, edited it, and mixed it with different sounds for each environment.

Animation & Assembly

We ran panels of the environment paintings through AfterEffects and turned them into looping subtle animations which would help to bring our story world to life. Then I wrote the content for the books of the bookstore while Dom put the final touches on the website.

takeaways

click anywhere to return to page

While Dom and I are very proud of what we were able to create with less than three months of work, we very much view Ribbonbound as a prototype. User surveys we took revealed some weaknesses in the navigability of the narrative, which we will remedy if we choose to take this story further someday.
