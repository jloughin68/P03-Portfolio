Ribbonbound

A multi-linear point-and-click experience with a painterly style and a whimsical urban fantasy theme, created by a team of two over three months. View it here.

the team

Dominic (Dom) Quon

  • Worldbuilding, organization
  • Environment painting, animation
  • HTML & JavaScript

Juliana Loughin

  • Worldbuilding, story mapping, dialogue writing, character design
  • Character & interactable object painting, animation
  • Music & sound design

pre-production

Concept

We adapted a story I had already written for film into an interactive medium: a post-apocalyptic, urban version of Hans Christian Anderson’s The Red Shoes, replete with hints of Irish folklore.

Bible

We created a detailed write-up specifying story world and characters; I led this process, as I was the practiced storyteller in the group, and love world building and character design.

Design Document

We compiled a detailed list of every environment, character, and object, their functionalities within the story, and the assets we would need to represent them.

Script

The script was my responsibility, as I love to write dialogue; writing Irvin’s lines in particular gave me a chance to be creative and whimsical.

Flow chart

In order to spatially represent the paths a player could take through the story, I drafted a flow chart and Dom made it presentable.

Concept Art

I created the character concept art, while Dom drafted the environments.

Storyboard

We adapted our flow chart in a visual, easy-to-parse way that acted as a rough first draft to our final work.

production

Painting

Dom worked with my feedback to refine the backgrounds, while, I painted the characters and objects. This was the most difficult portion of the project, as we had to meld together our two painting styles and match our lighting.

Sound

I arranged a medley of fiddle tunes, played it, recorded it, edited it, and mixed it with different sounds for each environment.

Code

While I was working on sound, Dom diligently worked at learning JavaScript in order to bring interactions to our story.

Animation & Assembly

We ran panels of the environment paintings through AfterEffects and turned them into looping subtle animations which would help to bring our story world to life. Then I wrote the content for the books of the bookstore while Dom put the final touches on the website.

takeaways

While Dom and I are very proud of what we were able to create with less than three months of work, we very much view Ribbonbound as a prototype. User surveys we took revealed some weaknesses in the navigability of the story, which we will remedy if we seek to update this story someday, which we very well may, as we believe we created an interesting story worth exploring further.
